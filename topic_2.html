What are the essential nutrients your body needs
every day?

Essential nutrients are vital for energy, growth, and overall health. These include carbohydrates, proteins, fats, vitamins, minerals, and water. Each plays a unique role in maintaining your body’s functions and preventing diseases.


In this section, we'll explore the importance of these nutrients, how much you need daily, and the best food sources. Understanding these basics can help you make healthier dietary choices and improve your well-being.

What are macronutrients?

Macronutrients are the nutrients your body needs in larger amounts to provide energy and support essential bodily functions. They include carbohydrates, proteins, and fats, each serving a unique and vital role in maintaining your health. Macronutrients also contain energy in the form of calories. Carbs are the main energy source, but your body can use other macronutrients for energy if needed.


The calorie content of each macronutrient is:

Carbs: 4 calories per gram

Protein: 4 calories per gram

Fat: 9 calories per gram

Examples of macronutrients

#1 Carbohydrates

Carbohydrates are the body's primary source of energy. They are broken down into glucose, which fuels your brain, muscles, and other vital tissues. Carbohydrates are found in a variety of foods, including:

• Grains: Bread, rice, pasta, oats

• Fruits: Apples, bananas, berries

• Vegetables: Potatoes, corn, peas

• Legumes: Beans, lentils, chickpeas

• Provide long-lasting energy

• Protect vital organs and maintain body temperature

• Support brain health and cell function

#2 Proteins

Proteins are essential for building and repairing tissues, making enzymes and hormones, and supporting immune function. They are made up of amino acids, which are the building blocks of protein. Sources of protein include:

01

Examples

• Poultry: chicken and turkey

• Eggs: particularly egg whites

• Red meat: beef, lamb, and pork

• Seafood: salmon, shrimp, and cod

• Dairy products: milk, yogurt, and cheese

02

Benefits

• Supports muscle growth and repair

• Helps in the production of enzymes and hormones

• Boosts immune function

#3 Fats

Fats are a concentrated source of energy and are important for many bodily functions, including cell structure, hormone production, and the absorption of fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, and K). Sources of fat include:

01

Examples

• Extra virgin olive oil

• Coconut: fresh, dried, and coconut oil

• Avocados: fresh and avocado oil

• Nuts and seeds: almonds and pumpkin seeds

02

Benefits

• Provide long-lasting energy

• Protect vital organs and maintain body temperature

• Support brain health and cell function

What are micronutrients?

Micronutrients are vitamins and minerals that your body needs in smaller amounts compared to macronutrients, but they are just as essential for maintaining health and preventing disease. Micronutrients play crucial roles in various bodily functions, including energy production, immune function, bone health, and blood clotting. Although they are needed in smaller quantities, their impact on your health is significant.

Why is hydration important and how much water should I drink daily?

Why is hydration important?

Hydration is essential for maintaining overall health and ensuring the proper functioning of the body. Water makes up about 60% of our body weight and plays a vital role in numerous physiological processes. It helps regulate body temperature through sweating and respiration, especially important in hot weather or during physical activity to prevent overheating. Water is also crucial for supporting cellular functions , as every cell, tissue, and organ relies on water for creation, maintenance, nutrient transport, and waste removal. Additionally, water aids digestion by helping break down food and moving it through the intestines, preventing constipation.


Hydration is important for skin health , as adequate water intake can improve skin elasticity, reduce dryness, and give the skin a healthy glow, while also preventing skin disorders and premature aging.

How much water should I drink daily?

The amount of water you need can vary based on several factors, including age, sex, weight, activity level, and climate. However, general guidelines can help ensure you stay properly hydrated:


1. General Recommendations:

The Institute of Medicine (IOM) recommends a daily water intake of about 13cups (3.7 liters) for men and 9 cups (2.7 liters) for women. For kids and teens should drink 6 to 8 cups of water a day. This includes all fluids consumed, not just water.


2. The 8x8 rule:

A common recommendation is to drink 8 glasses of water a day , which is about 2 liters. This is easy to remember and a good starting point.


3. Adjusting for Individual Needs:

- Activity Level: If you are physically active, you will need more water to compensate for fluid loss through sweat.

- Climate: Hot and humid climates increase the need for water intake to prevent dehydration.

- Health Conditions: Certain health conditions, such as kidney stones or urinary tract infections, may require increased water consumption. Pregnant or breastfeeding women also need more water.


4. Listening to Your Body:

- Thirst is a natural indicator that your body needs more water. However, relying solely on thirst may not be sufficient , especially for older adults, who may have a reduced sense of thirst.

- Monitoring urine colour can be a good indicator of hydration status. Light yellow or clear urine usually indicates proper hydration, while dark yellow urine suggests the need for more water.

How can you balance your diet to meet your health goals?

Balancing your diet to meet your health goals involves understanding your nutritional needs and making informed choices about the foods you eat. A balanced diet provides the right amounts of macronutrients and micronutrients necessary for optimal health.


Firstly, understand your nutritional needs. Your dietary requirements vary based on factors such as age, sex, weight, activity level, and health status. For example, athletes need more protein and carbohydrates, while older adults require more calcium and vitamin D. Consulting a healthcare provider or dietitian can help determine your specific needs and set realistic health goals.


Additionally, portion control and mindful eating. Even healthy foods can lead to weight gain if eaten in excess. Pay attention to portion sizes and eat meals and snacks at regular intervals to maintain energy levels and prevent overeating. Mindful eating, like eating slowly and savouring your food, helps you enjoy meals and make healthier choices.


Lastly, limit processed and high-sugar foods. Avoid foods high in added sugars, unhealthy fats, and sodium, such as sugary drinks and fast food. These can contribute to obesity, heart disease, and diabetes. Instead, opt for whole, minimally processed foods. When shopping, stick to the outer aisles of the store where fresh produce, dairy, and meats are usually located, and read ingredient lists on packaged foods.
